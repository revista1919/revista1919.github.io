Article Submission Policies

Editorial Protocol • San Felipe, Chile

01. Introduction

This document establishes the regulatory framework for submitting manuscripts to the National Review of Sciences for Students (ISSN 3087-2839). "Submission" is defined as any transfer of intellectual material through our official channels, based in San Felipe, Chile.

02. Clauses and Conditions

Regulatory Adherence Submitting the article implies full acceptance of the current Privacy Policy and Terms and Conditions of the journal.
Data Availability The author accepts that the form information and the manuscript will be available to the journal for editorial processing and potential public publication under the Creative Commons CC-BY license.
Round-Based Review Process The author commits to actively participating in the peer review process, addressing observations and suggestions within the reasonable deadlines established by the editorial committee. Revisions must align with the timeline suggested by the journal to ensure process efficiency.
Right to Retraction The author may request the retraction of their article at any stage prior to final publication. Once published, the retraction request will be evaluated by the editorial committee according to the Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE) guidelines.
Authorization for Minors If the author is a minor, they declare having obtained express consent from their legal guardian through the authorization form available at /en/login. The journal may request proof of such authorization at any time.
Review Rights The journal is granted a non-exclusive right to evaluation, correction, feedback, and issuance of technical reports.
Editorial Discretion The submission process does not constitute a promise of publication. Final acceptance depends solely on the peer committee and editors.
Ethical Responsibility The journal commits to rigorous review; the author assumes receiving professional criticism as part of academic growth.
Manuscript Iteration There are no resubmission limits for rejected articles, as long as they incorporate significant improvements after feedback.

03. Final Notes

Non-compliance with any of these points empowers the journal to dismiss the submission immediately. Transparency, rigor, and academic ethics are the pillars of our institution.
