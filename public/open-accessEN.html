Open Access Policies

Diamond Route · Version 1.0, July 2026 · San Felipe, Chile

01. Open Access Principles Statement

The National Review of Sciences for Students fully adheres to the global Open Access movement. We firmly believe that scientific knowledge, especially that generated by young researchers, must circulate freely, without economic, technical, or legal barriers, to maximize its social and academic impact.

Our commitment to open access is governed by the foundational definition of the Budapest Open Access Initiative (BOAI), which understands open access as free availability on the public internet, permitting any user to read, download, copy, distribute, print, search, or link to the full texts of the articles, without barriers other than those inherent to internet connectivity.

02. Funding Model: Diamond Open Access

The Journal operates under the Diamond Open Access model. This means that:

  • No Article Processing Charges (APCs) are levied: Authors do not have to pay absolutely any fee, charge, or cost for the submission, review, editing, typesetting, or publication of their articles. Publishing in the Journal is completely free for authors.
  • There are no subscription barriers for readers: All published content is immediately and freely available to any reader worldwide, without the need for registration, subscription, or payment. Access is universal and unrestricted from the moment of publication.
  • Internal and institutional funding: The Journal's operating costs are covered entirely by the institutional support of its sponsoring entities and the voluntary work of its editorial team and reviewers. No profit is pursued.

This model ensures that the economic capacity of the authors or their institutions is not a factor determining the possibility of publication, in full consonance with our principle of equity.

03. Publication License: Creative Commons CC BY 4.0

All articles published in the Journal, unless a legal or contractual obligation prevents it and this is expressly indicated in the article, are published under the Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0) license.

This is the most open and permissive open access license, recommended by leading open science initiatives such as Plan S and the Budapest Open Access Initiative's licensing guide. It maximizes the dissemination and reuse of knowledge.

3.1. What the CC BY 4.0 License Allows:

Share

Copy and redistribute the material in any medium or format for any purpose, even commercial.

Adapt

Remix, transform, and build upon the material for any purpose, even commercial.

3.2. What the CC BY 4.0 License Requires (Attribution):

Attribution (BY)

Appropriate credit must be given to the authors and the original source of publication (Journal name, volume, issue, DOI).

Indicate Changes

An indication must be given if changes were made to the original material.

No Additional Restrictions

Legal terms or technological measures that legally restrict others from doing anything the license permits may not be applied.

For more details about the license, visit the CC BY 4.0 license summary or the full legal code.

04. Copyright and Self-Archiving Policy

4.1. Copyright Ownership

Authors retain full copyright over their works, without any restriction. By submitting their manuscript and accepting its publication, authors grant the Journal only a non-exclusive license to publish the work for the first time. This license is royalty-free, perpetual, and worldwide in scope, and empowers the Journal to edit, typeset, publish, publicly communicate, preserve, and disseminate the article, as well as to assign it a Digital Object Identifier (DOI).

4.2. Self-Archiving Policy (No Embargo)

In accordance with our Diamond model and the CC BY 4.0 license, authors have the right to deposit the final published version of the article (Version of Record) in any repository or platform, immediately after publication, without any embargo period. This includes, but is not limited to:

  • Institutional repositories of their university or research center.
  • Subject repositories (such as arXiv, SocArXiv, RePEc, etc.).
  • Academic social networks (such as ResearchGate, Academia.edu).
  • Personal web pages or blogs.

The only condition is that the deposit includes the full bibliographic reference of the original publication (authors, title, Journal, volume, issue, year) and the article's DOI, as a permanent link to the Journal's version.

05. Digital Preservation and Persistent Identification

5.1. Persistent Identification (DOI)

Each accepted and published article receives a unique and persistent Digital Object Identifier (DOI), managed through Zenodo. The DOI guarantees the permanent online location of the article, even if the Journal's technological infrastructure changes in the future. The DOI is assigned to the article and registered in the international resolution systems (CrossRef/DataCite).

5.2. Digital Preservation Strategy

The Journal guarantees the long-term preservation of all its published content through a redundant archiving strategy:

  • Articles are stored and served from the Journal's official website.
  • A preservation deposit is made in Zenodo, a general-purpose repository maintained by CERN and the European Union, which guarantees the integrity and perpetual availability of the files.
  • Version control and backup are maintained in a public GitHub repository.

Commitment to permanence: In the unlikely event that the Journal ceases its editorial activities, access to all articles published to date will continue to be guaranteed through the aforementioned preservation repositories. The Journal reserves the right to modify or expand the repositories used, always with the aim of improving the security and accessibility of the academic record.

For any questions about these policies, please do not hesitate to contact the editorial team.
