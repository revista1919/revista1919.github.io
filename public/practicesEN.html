Best Practices

Standards for Authors, Reviewers, and Editors · Version 2.0, July 2026 · San Felipe, Chile

The integrity of the scientific literature depends on the ethical and professional conduct of all actors involved in the publication process. The National Review of Sciences for Students, committed to the highest standards of quality and transparency, has established the following code of best practices, based on the guidelines of the Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE), the International Committee of Medical Journal Editors (ICMJE), and other international reference organizations.

This code is binding on all participants in our editorial processes and complements our Editorial Policies.

01. Best Practices for Authors

1.1. Originality and Exclusivity

The Manuscript as an Original Contribution

The author undertakes to submit a work that constitutes an original contribution, written by themselves or by the declared team of co-authors. It has not been previously published in another medium with an ISSN/ISBN, nor is it being simultaneously considered by another publication. Any excerpt from prior work, whether their own or by others, is duly cited. Unauthorized translation or adaptation of a previously published work is considered a form of duplicate publication.

1.2. Responsible Authorship

Criteria and Transparency

All persons listed as authors meet the four ICMJE authorship criteria (substantial contribution, drafting or critical revision, final approval, and accountability). Contributions are detailed using the CRediT taxonomy. No person who meets the criteria has been excluded from the author list. Persons who have contributed in a secondary way are recognized in the acknowledgments, with their express consent. The order of authors is a unanimous consensus prior to submission.

1.3. Methodological Rigor and Intellectual Honesty

Data, Methods, and Accuracy

The data presented are real, complete, and have not been fabricated, falsified, or manipulated. Methodologies are described with sufficient precision to allow replication by other researchers. The study's limitations are explicitly acknowledged. Negative results or those that contradict the hypotheses are not omitted or embellished.

1.4. Accurate Citation and Acknowledgment of Sources

Avoiding Plagiarism in All Its Forms

All work by other researchers that has influenced the study, whether in the form of ideas, data, methods, or verbatim text, is explicitly and accurately acknowledged and cited. Self-plagiarism (unjustified textual recycling of prior works) is avoided. The use of generative AI tools is declared in accordance with the Journal's policy.

1.5. Transparency on Conflicts of Interest and Funding

Complete and Timely Declaration

All sources of funding, institutional support, and potential conflicts of interest (financial, personal, academic, or ideological) that may have influenced the work are declared in full in the manuscript and in the submission form.

1.6. Collaboration with the Editorial Process

Response and Correction

The corresponding author undertakes to manage communication with the Journal in an agile and professional manner, responding to the editors' requests within the established deadlines. If a significant error is detected in a published work, the authors are obliged to immediately notify the Journal and to collaborate in the publication of a correction (Corrigendum) or, in serious cases, in the retraction of the article.

02. Best Practices for Reviewers

2.1. Confidentiality

Protection of the Manuscript

The reviewer treats the manuscript and all associated materials as strictly confidential documents. They do not share them, discuss them with unauthorized third parties, or use the information contained therein for their own benefit or that of third parties prior to publication. The duty of confidentiality survives even after the conclusion of the review process.

2.2. Impartiality and Objectivity

Merit-Based Evaluation

The evaluation focuses exclusively on the scientific quality, originality, methodological rigor, and clarity of presentation of the manuscript. The reviewer avoids any bias related to the origin, affiliation, or personal characteristics of the authors (should they become aware of them). Criticisms are constructive, specific, and respectful, without offensive or derogatory language.

2.3. Declaration of Conflicts of Interest

Transparency on Potential Biases

The reviewer declares any real, potential, or apparent conflict of interest (recent collaborations, direct competition, personal relationships, etc.) that may compromise their impartiality. If the conflict is significant, they will decline the invitation to review.

2.4. Punctuality and Commitment

Meeting Deadlines

The reviewer accepts the invitation only if they can deliver a quality report within the established deadline. If unforeseen events arise, they communicate this to the editor as far in advance as possible. If they do not possess the necessary expertise to evaluate the manuscript, they decline the invitation immediately.

2.5. Responsible Use of Artificial Intelligence

Human Judgment Is Irreplaceable

The reviewer may use AI tools as a support to improve the writing or organization of their report, but may not delegate to them the critical reading or scientific judgment of the manuscript. It is prohibited to upload the manuscript, or substantial fragments thereof, to AI services that do not offer confidentiality guarantees, unless expressly authorized by the Journal. The ultimate responsibility for the report rests solely with the reviewer.

03. Best Practices for Editors

3.1. Editorial Independence

Decisions Based on Scientific Merit

The Editorial Board makes publication decisions based exclusively on the quality, originality, methodological rigor, and suitability to the Journal's scope. No commercial, institutional, political, or personal interest interferes with these decisions. The Journal does not apply publication quotas by institution, country, or theme.

3.2. Confidentiality and Transparency

Information Protection and Accountability

Editors protect the confidentiality of manuscripts and their review process, limiting access to those who require it for their functions. At the same time, policies, processes, and decision criteria are communicated openly and accessibly to all participants. Any substantial modification of editorial policies is notified transparently.

3.3. Management of Conflicts of Interest

Abstention and Case Handling

Editors refrain from participating in the management of manuscripts in which they have a real, potential, or apparent conflict of interest. When an undisclosed conflict is detected in an author or reviewer, an investigation is initiated in accordance with COPE flowcharts.

3.4. Handling of Scientific Misconduct

Investigation and Correction of the Record

In the face of allegations or well-founded suspicions of misconduct (plagiarism, data fabrication, image manipulation, undisclosed conflicts of interest, etc.), the Editorial Board acts diligently following COPE flowcharts and guidelines. If the investigation confirms the misconduct, the necessary corrective measures are applied: correction, expression of concern, or retraction of the article. The integrity of the academic record is an absolute priority.

3.5. Promotion of Diversity and Inclusion

Equity at All Stages

The Editorial Board actively works to promote diversity and inclusion in the composition of the editorial team, the selection of reviewers, and the evaluation of manuscripts. Any bias or discrimination based on gender, ethnic origin, nationality, sexual orientation, religious beliefs, or political views is avoided.

For any questions about this code or to report a potential breach, please contact the Editor-in-Chief at editor@revistacienciasestudiantes.com.
